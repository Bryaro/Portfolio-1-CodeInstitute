Benefit of cats

Teaching of being nice to all beings

"In every alms given to creatures who have a heart (heart) that is wet (live) will get a good reward. A Muslim who grows plants or herbs which are then eaten by birds, humans or animals, then for him as charity." - Authentic hadith narrated by Bukhari This hadith gives us an understanding that giving food or drink to animals is part of the charity and therefore whoever does this will get a reward from our creator Allah.

Train Us To Become Responsible Persons

In Islam, responsibility is one of the important aspects of daily life. By taking care of a cat, we will learn how to be responsible starting from providing food, water, providing a proper bed, to keeping the cat healthy.

Cleanliness and Purity

Islam places a strong emphasis on cleanliness and purity. Cats are considered clean animals, and their fur is not considered impure. In Islamic tradition, it is not required to wash a vessel that a cat has drunk from, unlike some other animals.

Protection from Harm

Keeping a cat in the house can bring blessings and protection from harm. It is said that cats have a natural ability to sense the presence of jinn (supernatural beings), and their presence can help guard against negative influences.
